About

I am an associate professor of information science at the University of Kentucky, where I teach in the library science and information, communication, and technology programs. My research interests include scholarly communication, information retrieval, and academic libraries.

Bio

I grew up in San Antonio, Texas and left for Monmouth College in Illinois upon the advice and encouragement of a high school English teacher. Monmouth was a great experience. I had wonderful professors, and I still think of them fondly.

Some life happened after college, and instead of going to graduate school like I thought I might, I started to work in restaurants full time. I cooked in restaurants in high school and some in college, and felt comfortable in that space. I also waited tables and tended bar and ended up working in about 21 restaurants overall (counting the jobs in H.S. and onward). I worked in fast food, a diner, a truck stop, a dive bar, various casual places, and one fine dining Italian restaurant in the Seattle area. I moved around a lot---something like 7 states and well over twice as many cities/towns.

I applied to graduate school and started in 2007. My intention was to become a librarian, but I was encouraged to apply to the PhD program. I think I would have enjoyed pursuing librarianship as a long-term career, but I am glad that I did the PhD. I've been at the University of Kentucky since 2013. It's not always an easy or enjoyable job. I think I am a line cook at heart, and the different cultures (restaurant and academia) create a kind of mental tension for me. However, it's a rewarding profession for the most part, and I'm pretty fortunate for it and for other things (like my family, which I never imagined I'd ever have!).

Thanks for visiting.
