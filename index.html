Sean Burns, PhD

Research

I am an assistant professor at the School of Information Science at the University of Kentucky. I primarily investigate problems related to scholarly communication and systems and issues related to academic libraries.

I am working collaboratively or solo on projects under the following, general topics:

  • bias in peer review
  • qualitative data archiving and preservation
  • megajournal publication patterns
  • information retrieval
  • history of library science

See my publications for more details.

Teaching

I teach online and face to face courses in our School's library science (LS) program and our information and communication technology (ICT) program.

For our graduate programs in LS and ICT, I teach courses on knowledge management, reference services, electronic resource management, and information seeking. For our undergraduate program in ICT, I teach courses on personal information/knowledge management, Linux systems administration, and semantic web development.
