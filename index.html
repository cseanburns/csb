Sean Burns, PhD

Research

I am an assistant professor of information science at the University of Kentucky. I primarily investigate problems related to scholarly communication and systems and issues related to academic libraries. I am working collaboratively or solo on projects under the following, general topics:

  • bias in peer review
  • qualitative data archiving and preservation
  • megajournal publication patterns
  • information retrieval
  • history of library science

Teaching

I teach undergraduate and graduate courses, online and face to face. At the undergraduate level, I teach courses on personal information and knowledge management, Linux systems administration, and semantic web development. At the graduate level, I teach courses on knowledge management, reference services, electronic resource management, and information seeking.
