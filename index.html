Research Updates

I am working on an information retrieval project related to bibliographic and abstracting/indexing databases. Other recent activities include:

  • Robert Shapiro, Tyler Nix, Jeff Huber and I had a talk accepted by the upcoming Medical Library Association conference. The title of the talk is Variation and Outliers in Search Results among MEDLINE-based Databases: A Longitudinal Study. The project is part of the MEDLINE research project mentioned below.
  • Robert Shapiro, Tyler Nix, Jeff Huber and I had a poster accepted at the 2019 iConference. The project examines discrepancies among MEDLINE databases. The title of the poster is Which MEDLINE? Outlier Search Results are Normal among MEDLINE Databases.
  • I am excited to participate in creating a workshop at the 2019 iConference with Marie Radford, Vanessa Kitzie, Diana Floegel, Lynn Silipigni Connaway, and Jenny Bossaller. The title of the workshop is InVivo Inspiration: Investigating Computer Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis Software (CAQDAS).
  • Chuck Fox and I concluded our three year project, and our fifth paper together, on bias in peer review with a publication in Scientometrics. Our previous work on peer review largely focused on gender bias, but this work focused on language, geography, and socioeconomics.
  • Jenny Bossaller, Amy VanScoy, and I published some work using qualitative secondary analysis as a methodology. This can be found in the Journal of Documentation.
  • Chuck Fox, Anna Muncy, Jennifer Meyer, and I published our third paper on gender bias in peer review. This one related to author suggested reviewers. The paper was published in Functional Ecology.
